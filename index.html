WHO ARE WE?

We are a student-run organization whose mission is to support local artists and creative initiatives through emerging and innovative blockchain technology.

WHY NFTS?

As a new, decentralized art form, NFTs are making art-making more accessible to anyone with enough money to mint one. Because of how much energy is necessary to mint an NFT, and because of the hype around blockchain tech, some NFTs have sold for millions of dollars. With NFTs, small artists have a chance to finally get rich quick - so naturally, most people who mint NFTs are not artists, and NFTs have a reputation for being aesthetically unpleasant. Even better, you can mint an NFT of any digital work on the internet, even if you do not own the rights to it.

WHAT DOES IT COST?

An analysis of 18,000 NFTs showed that the “average NFT has a carbon footprint… equivalent to more than a month’s worth of electricity for a person living in the EU.” This is due to the nature of cryptocurrency mining, specifically for Ethereum, which is the currency exchanged in most major NFT marketplaces: “[t]o keep financial records secure, the system forces people to solve complex puzzles using energy-guzzling machines.” For every mined crypto token, the cost of mining the next increases; if you have been unable to purchase a graphics card recently, chances are it’s due to blockchain enthusiasts buying out GPUs to feed their money-making machines. Also, artists have made their art accounts private or deleted them altogether to avoid having their work stolen.

WHAT IS IT WORTH?

Some NFTs have sold for millions, but their popularity is decreasing the more people realize that they’re not actually worth anything - an NFT is not an original copy of a digital work, but a link to a digital receipt that can be deleted or changed at any time. They’ve been used as a way to launder money and scam those looking to invest in new tech, though.
